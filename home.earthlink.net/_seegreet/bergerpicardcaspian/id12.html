Winter 2013/2014
Mom:
Caspian has been so very busy as the bee! We are full swing into Agility with one of the founders of Agility here in the USA - Julie Daniels!!! She is just SUPER!!! Cassie loves her and got to spend some time with her at her place while we were on vacation in December/January. He is coming along well and we expect to register for our first agility run later this Spring. Have also decided that Cassie will be retiring from being a stud dog. We are following a protocol which hopefully will help keep the Berger Picard gene pool healthy here in the USA. When too many of one line gets into the gene pool you can end up with what is known as the "Popular Sire Syndrome". Being that the Picard gene pool is still very small we need to make sure we do all that we can to diversify while maintaining all the good qualities. Cassie has had 19 beautiful, healthy puppies with outstanding temperaments. He has achieved the 5% requisite of having his line carried on. BIG Congratulations Caspian!!! One of Caspian's daughter's, (Izanne Aria de la Vie en Rose) was recently on a local television station. On youtube here:  Skip to about 17:25 into the program to see her - she is just BEAUTIFUL!!! Spring 2013  Health Note: Cassie will not be neutered unless there is a medical reason.

Mom:
Well, Cassie has earned his AKC Certificate of Merit!!! This is the only title currently available for the Berger Picard in the AKC. He did this by placing multiple times in first place against other breeds and also by competing against his own breed and finishing first - and once second =)

We were invited again for next year and every year thereafter! Bravo Cassie!
 
Summer
Mom:
Life seems to really get faster the older you get! Caspian has been a very busy boy! Firstly some big news about puppies - Caspian is a daddy again!
Married to a beautiful girl this past Spring 2013. How wonderful that there are more happy and healthy baby Picards! We here are hoping that there will be (at least) one that will pass all of the many qualifications to continue Caspian's line.
 
A note on health:
First are the eye tests, then in a couple of years at the earliest is when the big hip test is done to verify there is no hip dysplasia. Although we make sure every test is passed on both sides, there is, as of yet, no way to be 100% due to the environmental factor that is part of the health equation. Thank goodness Picards are blessed with such a few number of health issues and the ability of the internet that gives us all the ability to find wonderful responsible folks who care about their Picards and love them as much as we do our boy to make sure their dogs are health tested! Caspian is such a wonderful healthy full of sweetness boy. We all love him dearly!
 
Caspian also won a few more BIM group wins and we finally got to stay long enough to get our picture taken! But the big news from the end of August was the personal invitation to Quebec from our last show. The judge said Caspian was "Just beautiful!" and she would really like us to come up Quebec where she can judge him with the other Picards up there as she has judged them before. What a compliment! There is one in September next year with judges from France I believe. So that is the plan for next year by the latest. Also we are getting Caspian registered with the CKC (Canadian Kennel Club) so that we may show him in Canada at the various CKC shows. Caspian's next trip will be this coming October at the first annual meeting the club will have as the AKC parent club. Am so excited about seeing all the wonderful Picards that will be there and the many folks we know and love!

April
Mom:
Congratulations Champion Caspian de Lann Guic! Our very first UKC show this weekend, Cassie started and finished earning his UKC Conformation Champion title! We had a wonderful time! He even had a group of admirers whistling and cheering him on during our final event. It made our whole first show experience unforgettable! We learned so much, and again have some idea of how much I know I do not know. One of the most important things I still have to learn is where in the group line up we should be (no one seemed to have any idea, and we almost ran into the dogs in front of us a couple of times). I would have never thought about tearing the ends of the armband to get the elastic band to stay on (because mine kept falling off)! Or that I really need to get a dressy pair of real running shoes so that the judge can see how Cassie gates without being held back (because I kept feeling like my shoe was going to fall off my foot). I had absolutely no idea that Cassie prefers day old warm cheese to fresh roast beef as bait when he always prefers roast beef at home (that was a lucky find)! Cassie should also not ever have a hose down during the evening before the show (his fur gets funny looking cowlicks as it dries from sleeping); the list can go on and on (it all looks so easy on the TV, you tube, and was very different while we were practicing). I cannot imagine what it would have been like if we had not been practicing or attending Conformation class (well actually I could I think, but its not a pretty thought)! It seems like in so many other aspects of life, there is nothing like just getting in there and experiencing things first hand. So I think we most certainly will keep going to shows when we get the opportunity. It was a lot of fun, and exciting, and we really really enjoyed the learning process! I feel that Cassie and I actually know each other better too! There were so many folks who were just wonderfully helpful and nice (actually, I do not think we could have done what we did without their help and advice). I want to especially thank, for a great number of reasons and help, Marsha and her menagerie =) Her Briard, Slipper is one of the most beautiful dogs I have ever seen. We also found out that she and Marsha were at the specialty in Kentucky last September (where we were too)! Slipper received an award of merit - she was one of only 6 dogs who recieved it in a group of 50 entered champion Briards. We did get to meet so many super dogs, and we even got a recipe for a superb chocolate cake! You can see a video of the show that my son made for us here. Congratulations again Cassie!!!
 
Also, Chanel is due to have her puppies on May 5th! This is the second time Chanel has had AI (the first time was not with Cassie) with both pregnancies resulting in only two puppies each. I am now and will be continually thankful that they look good and healthy, and will continue to be so after they are born. Will keep updating here as events unfold. Stay tuned!

The veterinarian thinks that Whistle had uterine inertia, (where the cervix opened but there were no contractions to push the pups out). There were three beautiful boys and one precious girl. They were buried next to a creek under the branches of a willow tree. It is comforting to know that mommy Whistle is doing okay.
