French Cotation explained:

Cotation/Valeur 1 - Confirme (Issue of permanent pedigree) The dog is recognized as conforming to the Standard of the Breed by an accredited judge. The birth certificate is then transformed to a pedigree by the French Kennel Club.

Cotation/Valeur 2 - Premier Choix The confirmed dog has obtained the grade of at least one 'Excellent' in morphology (conformation) at a dog show and passed the Test de Caractère (Temperament Test) at the Berger Picard Nationale d'Elevage.

Cotation/Valeur 3 - Excellent The confirmed dog has obtained at least one 'Excellent' rating in morphology at a Picard Specialty show (SPE), Régionale d'Elevage (RE) or Nationale d'Elevage Specialty (NE) or the French Championship Show (CHPT) and Excellent in character at a Berger Picard Régionale d'Elevage, or Nationale d'Elevage Specialty level exposition, AND has been tested for Hip Dysplasia (HD) with a grade of A, B, or C. Or, have a Very Good (TB) in a SPE, RE, CHPT or NE , plus Herding Test or Working Diploma.

Cotation/Valeur 4 - Recommande The confirmed dog has obtained at least 2 'Excellent' ratings in morphology in a SPE, RE, CHPT, or NE by two different judges. An 'Excellent' rating in Test de Caractère Herding or Working Diploma. Plus a HD grade of A, B, or C. Plus a clean eye exam.

Cotation/Valeur 5 - Elite B The confirmed dog is not itself titled Recommande, but it is not dysplastic and has produced or sired the required number of titled offspring in one generation of:

Males: 5 descendants worth 3 points with 2 dams.
Female: 5 descendants worth 3 points in 2 litters.

Cotation/Valeur 6 - Elite A The confirmed dog has obtained the title of Recommande (4/6 in Cotation/Valeur) and has produced or sired the required number of titled offspring in one generation of:

Males: 5 descendants worth 3 points with 2 dams.
Female: 5 descendants worth 3 points in 2 litters.

**It is of interest to note that in each show when the evaluations are done, each dog is scanned or otherwise confirmed to ascertain that it is the same dog as the paper says it is. Also, a dog who is imported to another country from France (such as the USA), as have many puppies in the last several years, without having acquired any of the cotation marks from France has only a special "Export Pedigree", even as an adult and cannot be used for breeding registered puppies in France. Even multi-titled champion status here has no weight there. Caspian for example, being imported as an 8 week old puppy has only this special "Export Pedigree", even though he is titled, has a hip grade of 'B', passed his eye exam, passed two temperament tests (plus heart, thyroid and dentition) and received an 'Excellent' in morphology/conformation evaluation at the American Élevage Berger Picard Specialty from the French Berger Picard Club President and judge Mr. Larive.
